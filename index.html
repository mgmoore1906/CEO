A four-year economic mobility model aligning Career & Technical Education, industry-based certifications, entrepreneurship, workforce readiness, and structured student support into one coherent pathway.

The education-to-workforce pipeline is still fragmented

Students often encounter disconnected experiences: CTE coursework in one lane, certifications in another, work-based learning somewhere else, and little sustained support tying those efforts into real economic mobility. CEO is built to close those gaps intentionally.

  • See the model

CEO integrates the pieces that usually sit apart

Our approach brings together certification acceleration, entrepreneurship, workforce readiness, and structured well-being support inside one long-term cohort model. The result is a stronger bridge between aspiration, preparation, and verified opportunity.

Program Model

CEO is built as a multi-year architecture for economic mobility. Students move through a coordinated system designed to build skills, confidence, social capital, and measurable outcomes over time — not through one-off experiences.

Certification Accelerator

Preparation for industry-based credentials aligned to high-value career pathways and postsecondary momentum.

Entrepreneurship Lab

Structured venture exploration, idea testing, and business formation experiences rooted in evidence and execution.

Workforce Readiness

Employer exposure, professional skill-building, work-based learning, and stronger navigation into college and career.

Well-Being & Resilience

Student support structures that reinforce consistency, reflection, resilience, and long-term performance.

Stakeholder Alignment

School districts, employers, postsecondary institutions, and philanthropy working from a more coordinated playbook.

Outcomes That Matter

Certification attainment, postsecondary persistence, career-connected experience, and stronger economic mobility indicators.
