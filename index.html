Cohort for Economic Opportunity

A four-year commitment to economic mobility — aligning Career & Technical Education, industry-based certifications, entrepreneurship, workforce readiness, and structured well-being support into one integrated cohort model.

Launching in Houston, Texas. Built for replication.

The education-to-workforce pipeline is fragmented

Millions of students participate in CTE programs, yet too few transition into certification-aligned employment, postsecondary completion, or sustainable entrepreneurial ventures. CEO integrates education, employers, and workforce systems intentionally — not episodically.

What makes CEO different

A four-year cohort commitment (sophomore year through postsecondary transition), embedded employer validation, CTE-forward but not pathway-locked support, and entrepreneurship as incubation — evidence over enthusiasm.

Program model

Across four years, students progress through integrated layers that build skills, confidence, social capital, and verified outcomes. Technology enables the system — people deliver the differentiation.

Entrepreneurship Lab

Skill-based venture incubation: discovery, feasibility, viability, MVP testing, and reflection.

CTE Certification Accelerator

Cohort-based preparation aligned to H3 careers with applied labs and readiness validation.

Career & Workforce Readiness

Professional identity formation, employer exposure, internships, and postsecondary navigation.

Well-Being & Resilience

Structured stress regulation and resilience support — performance infrastructure, not therapy.

Outcomes that matter

  • Increased attainment of industry-based certifications
  • Improved postsecondary enrollment, persistence, and completion
  • Certification-aligned employment and work-based learning placements
  • Validated entrepreneurial pilots and revenue-generating ventures (where appropriate)
  • Durable employer and community partnerships supporting local talent pipelines
