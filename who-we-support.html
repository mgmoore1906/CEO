Who We Support

CEO is designed to serve multiple stakeholders through one integrated, four-year cohort model. Choose your role below to see what partnership looks like.

  • School Districts
  • Employers
  • Funders
  • Families & Students

School Districts

CEO strengthens CTE outcomes by pairing cohort-based programming with a durable system for certification tracking, skill documentation, and employer-connected learning.

  • Integrated model: entrepreneurship + certifications + workforce readiness + resilience support
  • Measurable outcomes: attainment, persistence, placements, and portfolio artifacts
  • Implementation clarity: playbooks that support replication across campuses/pathways
  • Start a district conversation

Employers

Employers plug in as validators, not instructors—confirming skills align to real job expectations and helping students build credible social capital.

  • Skill validation: review artifacts, confirm standards, and strengthen credibility
  • Real exposure: panels, site visits, job shadows—high value, low burden
  • Pipeline development: early visibility into emerging talent with verified skills
  • Become an industry partner

Funders

CEO is designed to move beyond short-term interventions. Funding supports a four-year cohort commitment with measurable outcomes across certifications, placement, and mobility.

  • Fundable metrics: pass rates, completion, drop-off reduction, placements, portfolios
  • Houston pilot: launch locally with a model built for replication
  • Governance-minded: student privacy, ethical data use, and outcome transparency
  • Support a cohort

Families & Students

Students advance through a four-year cohort journey that builds identity, skills, and real-world readiness—with structured support to persist through pressure and uncertainty.

  • Four-year progression: identity → validation → specialization → transition
  • Documented progress: certifications + portfolio artifacts + employer exposure
  • Clear boundaries: well-being support is skills-based and consent-driven
  • Express student interest
